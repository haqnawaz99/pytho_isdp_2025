## Multiple choice

1. `25 // 3` equals:
   - a) 8.33  
   - b) 8  
   - c) 1  

2. `25 % 3` equals:
   - a) 8  
   - b) 1  
   - c) 3  

3. `10 + 5 * 3` equals:
   - a) 45  
   - b) 25  
   - c) 30  

4. `marks >= 70` gives:
   - a) int  
   - b) bool  
   - c) str  

5. `True and False` is:
   - a) True  
   - b) False  
   - c) Error  

6. `f"{5 * 4}"` prints:
   - a) `{5 * 4}`  
   - b) `20`  
   - c) `5 * 4`  

**Answers:** 1-b, 2-b, 3-b, 4-b, 5-b, 6-b

---

## Short answer

1. When do we use `//` instead of `/`?  
2. What is the difference between `and` and `or`?

**Sample:**  
1. When we need a whole-number share (items, students, shelves).  
2. `and` needs both True; `or` needs at least one True.
